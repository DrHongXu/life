Currently, Dr. Hong Xu serves as an R&D Engineer specializing in e-Power Systems based in Munich, dedicated to achieving carbon neutrality in the Energy and Transport sectors. Throughout his career, he has worked with international corporations and academic institutions in Europe and China, achieving over 15 published papers with nearly 300 citations, and 3 granted patents. Moreover, he chaired a session at the UN Climate Change Conference in Bonn, participated Group of Experts at the UN Economic Commission for Europe based in Geneva, and served as an Academic Jury at the St. Gallen Symposium in Switzerland. Dr. Xu is also a Peer Reviewer for several high-impact journals in the Energy, Transport, and Sustainability fields. His interdisciplinary efforts have been recognized with awards including the Santander Bank Scholarship, ECS Invited Paper and Travel Grant, Intel Edge AI Scholarship, Erasmus Scholarship, and etc.

He pursued his PhD in Renewable Energy from ETH Zurich, in collaboration with the Paul Scherrer Institute in Switzerland. Prior to that, he received a Master's Degree in Materials Science from the Technical University of Munich with internships at the European XFEL and Infineon in Germany. Dr. Xu's passion for impactful research has led to long-term engagement with industry leaders in hydrogen and fuel cells, including Toyota Motor in Japan and Freudenberg Group in Germany. In parallel, he pursued an MBA at the Quantic School of Business and completed Executive Courses at the Cambridge Judge Business School, gaining practical experience at a dual-currency venture capital firm and a leading securities company in China.
